Troubleshooting & Maintenance

Common problems and fixes, plus basic consumables and maintenance pointers.

1Troubleshooting

The most common beginner problems and what to check first. If a fix involves gas plumbing, electronics, or you're unsure — stop and ask your PI or call SRI tech support (number below).

SymptomLikely cause & what to try
"Acquisition system is not functioning"PeakSimple can't reach the GC. Check the USB cable, that the GC is powered on, and that Edit > Overall has the right board (302) and COM/USB port.
No peaks at allThe valve may not have injected — check the event table relay letter and time, and that sample gas is actually flowing to the loop. Confirm you're viewing the right channel. For the FID side, make sure the flame is lit.
FID channel is dead/flatFlame likely went out. Confirm H₂ and air, then re-ignite per your lab's procedure. The "fog on a cold wrench" test confirms a lit flame.
Baseline drifting or noisyUsually the GC isn't thermally stable yet — wait for setpoints. Persistent drift/noise can mean a leak, contamination, or a column needing bake-out. Tell your PI.
Peaks appear but aren't namedThe peak fell outside its retention window. Drag the window over it (Section 7) and press Enter. If retention times shifted, check carrier pressure and oven temperature match the method.
Retention times keep shiftingCarrier pressure or oven program changed. Reload the master .con so conditions are identical, and let temperatures stabilize.
Concentrations look wrongCalibration is stale or for a different range. Re-run standards and rebuild/refresh the curve (Section 8). Check the units in the component match your standard.
"Can't enter this calibration level"Levels must be in ascending/descending order for both concentration and area. A point out of order usually means a bad standard run — re-run rather than force it.
Results window is emptyNo components defined, or the channel isn't integrating. Check Edit > Channels → the Integrate box for that channel, and that the component table is loaded.
Extra / ghost peaksCarryover from a previous injection or contamination. Run a blank, check valve timing, and confirm the loop is fully flushed between runs.
ℹSRI Tech Support SRI is genuinely responsive by phone: (310) 214-5092. Have your GC model (8610C) and a description of the symptom ready.

2Maintenance & Consumables

A GC needs routine upkeep to keep giving trustworthy numbers. This is a basic overview of the parts that wear out or get consumed — what they are, how you know they need attention, and where the full replacement procedure lives. Always follow the SRI manual or call tech support for the actual procedure; several involve hot zones, hydrogen, and delicate parts.

ItemWhat & whySigns it needs attentionProcedure
H₂ supply / generatorHydrogen runs the carrier, TCD reference and FID flame; a generator makes it on demand (safer than a cylinder).Pressure won't hold; spent generator desiccant; rising baseline noise.Generator manual; SRI tech support.
Gas filters / moisture & O₂ trapsTraps on the carrier line keep oxygen and water off the columns and TCD — the single biggest thing protecting column life.Indicating trap changes colour; baseline drift; shifting retention; lost sensitivity.Replace when spent (manual); never let them run out.
Internal air-compressor filterSupplies clean air to the FID flame. SRI kit 8670-1070 (particle filter + tubing + connectors).Weak/erratic FID flame; compressor noise; visibly dirty filter.SRI "Air Compressor Filter Kit" doc + SRI GC Training YouTube channel.
Methanizer catalystNickel catalyst that converts CO/CO₂ to methane for the FID; finite life and can be poisoned.CO/CO₂ peaks shrink or vanish on the methanizer channel while the TCD still sees them.SRI methanizer (510-series) kit; replace per SRI — call tech support.
Gas-sampling-valve rotorThe rotating seal inside each GSV wears with cycles.Irreproducible peak areas; leaks; sticky/again actuation.Valco / SRI valve actuator manuals; tech support.
Columns (packed)Your MolSieve 5A / HayeSep D / Alumina columns separate the gases; they degrade with age and O₂/water exposure.Tailing or broadening peaks, shifting retention, poor resolution that bake-out won't fix.SRI "Column Installation" doc. Note: packed columns cannot be solvent-rinsed (only capillary columns can) — they're replaced.
Injector septum & ferrulesThe on-column injection port uses a septum and graphite ferrules that wear.Inlet leaks, ghost peaks, pressure won't hold.SRI manual; replace as needed.
ℹWhere the full procedures live This page is intentionally brief. For step-by-step replacement use the 8610C Manual — Part 8 (Maintenance) and the specific SRI documents (Air Compressor Filter Kit, methanizer kit, Column Installation, valve actuator manuals) on the SRI documents & downloads page, the SRI GC Training YouTube channel, and SRI Tech Support (310) 214-5092.
⚠Before any maintenance Cool hot zones, manage hydrogen safely (shut it at the source before opening the flow path), and never stop carrier to a hot column. When in doubt, stop and ask your PI or SRI.

3Glossary

TermPlain-English meaning
Chromatogram (.CHR)The signal-vs-time plot a detector produces during a run — the picture full of peaks.
PeakA bump in the chromatogram. One gas = one peak. Its area is proportional to how much of that gas there is.
Retention timeHow long (minutes) a gas takes to travel through the column and reach the detector. Identifies what the peak is.
Retention windowThe bracket you set around a peak so PeakSimple knows "the peak in here is CO."
BaselineThe zero-line under the peaks. Integration draws it; a clean baseline gives accurate areas.
IntegrationMeasuring the area under each peak.
Carrier gasThe gas that continuously pushes your sample through the column. On this GC it is hydrogen, which also serves as the TCD reference and the FID fuel.
EPCElectronic Pressure Control — PeakSimple/GC sets carrier pressure electronically for reproducibility.
Gas-sampling valve (GSV)A valve with a fixed-volume loop that injects a repeatable puff of your gas stream. Moved by a relay.
RelayAn on/off switch PeakSimple controls (named A–H). OFF/ON moves the valve between LOAD and INJECT.
Event table (.EVT)The timed to-do list for a run — e.g. "turn relay A ON at 0.10 min" to inject.
Control file (.con)The full saved method: temperatures + events + components + calibrations bundled together.
Component table (.CPT)Your list of named peaks, their windows, and calibration curves.
Calibration curve (.CAL)The area-vs-concentration relationship built from known standards; converts area → real concentration.
ChannelOne detector's data stream. On your GC: Channel 1 = FID, Channel 2 = methanizer (CO/CO₂ as methane), Channel 3 = TCD.
TCD / FID / MethanizerYour two detectors (universal / hydrocarbon-sensitive) and the catalyst that lets the FID see CO & CO₂ as methane.
