Research Areas

Three Frontiers of Catalytic Science

01

CO₂ Hydrogenation to Plastics, Chemicals & Fuels

Novel dual-functional catalysts to selectively produce hydrocarbons from CO₂ via RWGS and Fischer-Tropsch. Recent work establishes β-W₂C as a phase-selective, high-performance RWGS catalyst rivaling platinum at a fraction of the cost.

RWGS · FT · Tungsten Carbide
02

Selective Synthesis of Light Olefins from Syngas

Direct CO/H₂ conversion via Fischer-Tropsch to Olefins (FTO), combining mesoporous supports, promoter engineering, and in situ XAFS/FTIR spectroscopy to reveal structure–activity relationships beyond the ASF distribution.

FTO · XAFS · In Situ Spectroscopy
03

Accelerating Decarbonization with AI & LLMs

Pioneering representation of catalysts using the text of synthesis procedures and reaction conditions, combined with Bayesian optimization — enabling discovery of earth-abundant active catalysts without expensive structural characterization.

LLMs · Bayesian Optimization · Trimetallic Catalysts

ACS Catalysis
January 2026

Achieving Phase Control of Polymorphic Tungsten Carbide Catalysts

Perera, S.M.H.D. · Ciuffetelli, E. · Porosoff, M.D.

By maintaining particle sizes below 10 nm and using temperature-programmed carburization, the team selectively synthesized β-W₂C — establishing it as an active and stable catalyst for the reverse water-gas shift reaction. This resolves longstanding phase ambiguities in tungsten carbide catalysis. Funded by the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation and DOE Office of Basic Energy Sciences.

Intrinsically Bifunctional Tungsten Carbide Enables Efficient PVC-Compatible Polyolefin Hydrocracking

Nwachukwu, U.C. · Moegling, M.J. · Perera, S.M.H.D. · … · Deshpande, S. · Porosoff, M.D. · Chen, L.

In collaboration with Linxiao Chen (Univ. North Texas) and UR's Siddharth Deshpande, the group demonstrated that phase-controlled tungsten carbide drives polyolefin hydrocracking more than 10× more efficiently than conventional platinum — and resists deactivation by PVC contaminants that kill noble-metal catalysts. Funded by NSF.

Leveraging and Understanding Exotherms in Tandem Catalysts with In Situ Luminescence Thermometry

Perera, S.M.H.D. · Harrington, B. · Fadhul, A. · Pickel, A.D. · Porosoff, M.D.

Using upconverting nanoparticle (UCNP)-based luminescence thermometry with the Pickel lab, the team measured catalyst surface temperatures directly — finding 10–100°C discrepancies vs. bulk readings. Research Program

Our Research

The Porosoff group focuses on developing new catalysts for upgrading C1 and C2 resources for efficient energy storage and low-cost production of plastics, chemicals and fuels. Understanding the relationships between chemical reactivity and catalyst electronic/structure properties requires controlled synthesis, in situ techniques, and theoretical calculations.

01

CO₂ Hydrogenation to Plastics, Chemicals & Fuels

Through catalytic reactions, waste CO₂ can be converted to oxygenates, specialty chemicals and hydrocarbons. We investigate dual-functional catalysts to selectively produce a tight distribution of hydrocarbons from CO₂ via RWGS followed by Fischer-Tropsch synthesis. Recent work demonstrates β-W₂C as a phase-selective, active RWGS catalyst with performance rivaling platinum.

RWGS · FT · Tungsten Carbide · Phase Control
02

Selective Synthesis of Light Olefins from CO and H₂

Light olefins (C₂–C₄) are critical raw materials for plastics and fuels. We explore direct Fischer-Tropsch to Olefins (FTO), combining mesoporous high-surface-area supports with carefully selected promoters. In situ XAFS and FTIR spectroscopy probe the effect of promoters on catalyst structure and electronic properties — going beyond the Anderson-Schulz-Flory distribution.

FTO · XAFS · FTIR · Promoter Engineering
03

Accelerating Decarbonization with AI & Language Models

Rather than relying on expensive structural and electronic characterization, we leverage large language models (LLMs) to represent catalysts by the text of synthesis procedures and reaction conditions. Combined with Bayesian optimization, this accelerates discovery of earth-abundant, active catalysts for RWGS and beyond — with a focus on trimetallic systems that are otherwise difficult to characterize.

LLMs · NLP · Bayesian Optimization · Trimetallic Catalysts
